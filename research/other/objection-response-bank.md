# Objection Response Bank

Extracted from Connor (Higher Levels), Taylor Haren, and Y Combinator guidance.

## Common Objections + Response Patterns

| # | Objection | Response Pattern |
|---|-----------|------------------|
| 1 | "We already have a solution" | "Totally understand. What does that tool not cover that keeps you up at night?" |
| 2 | "No budget right now" | "Makes sense. Can we still set 15 minutes so when priorities shift, you're ready?" |
| 3 | "Not a priority" | "Got it. Is [recent company event] on your roadmap this quarter?" |
| 4 | "Send me more info" | "Happy to. To make it relevant, what's the #1 gap you're trying to solve?" |
| 5 | "Too busy" | "No problem. Would next week or the week after work better?" |
| 6 | "We build in-house" | "Cool. What stack are you using? I ask because most teams we work with switched from [similar tool] after hitting [specific limitation]." |
| 7 | "Compliance/legal blocks" | "Understood. Many of our finance/healthcare clients had the same concern. We're SOC2 / [cert]. Worth a quick chat on how we handle it?" |
| 8 | "Wrong person" | "Thanks for the redirect. Who handles [relevant area] now?" |
| 9 | "Never heard of you" | "No worries — we work with [3 comparable logos]. What's the best way to share a 2-min overview?" |
| 10 | "Just go off my list" | "Will do. Before I do — is it the timing or the fit? Feedback helps." |

---

## Response Rules
1. Never be defensive
2. Always pivot back to a meeting or a qualifying question
3. A "no" is better than silence — it gives you data
